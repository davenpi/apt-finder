# Apartment Search Preferences

Write your preferences in natural language. The evaluation agents read this file verbatim, so be specific about what matters to you. Think of it as briefing a friend who's apartment hunting on your behalf.

## Budget

- **Target**: $X,XXX/mo
- **Ceiling**: $X,XXX (absolute max you'd consider)
- **Broker fee**: Your stance on fees (no-fee only, fee acceptable, etc.)

## Location

- **Neighborhood**: Where you're looking
- **Street preference**: Any preferences on quiet vs. busy streets, avenues vs. side streets
- **Orientation**: Preference for street-facing, rear-facing, courtyard, etc.

## Unit Requirements

- **Type**: 1 BR, Studio, 2 BR, etc.
- **Must-haves**: Anything that's a hard requirement (e.g. in-unit W/D, dishwasher, elevator)
- **Nice-to-haves**: Things you'd prefer but can live without

## What Matters Most

Rank what you care about. The agents use this to score listings, so be honest about priorities. Examples:

1. **Natural light** — How important is it? Do you work from home?
2. **Bathroom condition** — Is a grimy bathroom a dealbreaker or just a negative?
3. **Kitchen quality** — Do you cook a lot? Counter space, gas vs. electric?
4. **Noise** — How sensitive are you? Ground floor vs. high floor?
5. **Finishes** — Modern renovation vs. character/charm vs. don't care?
6. **Value** — How price-sensitive are you within your range?

## Red Flags

List things that should count against a listing:

- Missing photos (which rooms?)
- High days on market
- "Representative" photos (not the actual unit)
- Specific dealbreakers (e.g. no window in bedroom, 5th floor walkup)

## Logistics

- **Move-in date**: When do you need to be in?
- **Pets**: Any?
- **Commute**: Office location or fully remote?
- **Building type**: Doorman, walkup, elevator, no preference?
